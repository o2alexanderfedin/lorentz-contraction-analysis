This device demonstrates how simultaneity is detected in special relativity:

  • Central Detector: Located at the midpoint between two sensors
  • Light Signals: Events create light signals that travel at speed c
  • Simultaneity Test: Events are simultaneous if their light signals arrive at the central detector at the same time
  • Frame Dependence: Change the observer velocity to see how simultaneity becomes relative

Key Insight: Two events that appear simultaneous in one reference frame may not be simultaneous in another frame moving relative to the first. This is the relativity of simultaneity - a fundamental aspect of special relativity that leads to effects like length contraction.
